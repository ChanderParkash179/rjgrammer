Parts Of Speech

Noun

A noun is a naming word. It is used to name a person, place , thing, animal or idea.

For Example:
book , girl, month, city, Lahore, Pakistan, Monday, July, bat, ball, water, tree, clouds, truth, milk, sugar, water, knowledge, gas etc.
Pronoun

Pronouns are the words used in place of nouns.

For Example:
he, she, it, they, them, we, us, yourself, myself, herself, yours, mine, ours, its, hers, theirs, each other, one another, which, that etc.
Verb

a word used to describe an action, state, or occurrence, and forming the main part of the predicate of a sentence

For Example:
hear, become, happen, run, listen etc.
Adjective

Adjectives are describing words. They are used to modify a noun.

For Example:
Blue bottle, beautiful flower, two pens, many girls etc.
Adverb

An adverb is a word that modifies a verb, an adjective and an other adverb.

For Example:
She sang loudly ( loudly modifies the verb sang)
You are quite right. (the adverb quite modifies the adjective right)
She sang very loudly (the adverb very modifies the adverb loudly) etc.
Preposition

Preposition indicates the relation of a noun or pronoun to the rest of sentence.

For Example:
in, on, about, over, under, during, at, up down, into etc.
Conjunction

Conjunctions are the joining words. They are used to join two parts of a sentence.

For Example:
and, but, or, nor, so, far, yet, either, neither, rather, than etc.
Interjection

Interjection are used to express strong feelings or sudden emotions. They are included in a sentence to express a sentiment, such as surprise, disgust, joy or excitement.

For Example:
Hey! Hurrah! Wow! What a surprise! Aww!
Articles

The indefinite article is “a” or “an”
It refers to one of a type
It refers to something for the first time.
and
The definite article is “the”
It is used to refer to
The things that is unique
To general class
To something that has been mentioned previously

For Example:
a book, an egg, an honest man, a unique idea etc.
the Himalayas, the great wall of china, the moon, the sun. the poor, the rich etc.
