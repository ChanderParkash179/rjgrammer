Active and Passive Voice

What is Voice?

The verb of Sentences tells the voice. It tells us that subject of the Sentences receives or performs the action.

Example:
She prepares dinner.
Dinner is prepared by her
Active Voice:

In active voice the subject is in the focus and performs the action indicated by the verb.
Usage: It is used to show a direct relation between subject and verb when more clarity is required.

Passive Voice:

In passive voice the object is in the focus and receives the action indicated by the verb.
Usage: When action is the focus and does is not known we use passive voice.

Active Passive Voice by Tenses

Present Simple Tense

Formula: (Is/Am/Are) + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle is lighted by her
Present Continuous/Progressive Tense

Formula: (Is/Am/Are) + being + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle is being lighted by her.
Present Perfect Tense

Formula: (Has/Have) + been + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle has been lighted by her.
Past Simple Tense

Formula: (Was/Were) + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle was lighted by her.
Past Continuous/Progressive Tense

Formula: (Was/Were) + being + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle was being lighted by her
Past Perfect Tense

Formula: (Had) + been + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle had been lighted by her
Future Simple Tense

Formula: (Will) + be + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle will be lighted by her
Future Perfect Tense

Formula: (Will) + have been + 3rd form of Verb

Example:
Active Voice: She lights the candle.
Passive Voice: The candle will have been lighted by her
Note:

Passive voice cannot be formed for the following tenses.
Present Perfect Continuous
Past Perfect Continuous
Future Continuous
Future Perfect Continuous
Reason : It causes meaninglessness in the sentence and rules of transitivity does not allow these tenses to be used as passive voice.

E.g. He will be meeting us next week.
We will be being met by him next week
