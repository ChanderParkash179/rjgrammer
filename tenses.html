Tenses

Present Tense

Present Simple Tense
Present Simple Tense is used to state an action which occurs on regular basis in present. These actions include Habitual or repeated actions, scheduled actions occuring on regular basis; and facts that stand true all time.
Formula: Subject + 1st form of Verb + Object
He/She/It/Name ==> Does
I/We/You/They/Names ==> Do
Example:
He works in a factory
She loves her kids.
you do work.
Present Continuous/Progressive Tense
Present Continuous Tense is used to describe a continued or an on-going action fo the present. These actions are occurring exactly at the time of speaking. It is called progressive tense because it refers to actions which are currently in progress at the time of speaking.
For instance, a boy says, "I am drinking water.". It means that the action (drinking water) is being done exactly at this time of the speaking.
This Tense is also called Present Progressive Tense.
Formula : Subject + Is/Am/Are + 1st form of Verb + ing + Object
He/She/It/Names ==> Is
I ==> Am,
You/We/Names ==> Are
Example:
I am drinking water
we are working
He is playing
Present Perfect Tense
Present Perfect Tense is used to express an action that occurred or completed at some point in past. This tense expresses an idea of completion or occurrence of an action in past without giving an idea about the exact time of its occurrence. Though, the time of the action is not exactly known, this tense is mostly used to refer to actions completed in the recent past (not a very long time ago).
Formula: Subject + Have/Has + 3rd Form of Verb + Object
He/She/It/Name ==> Has
I/We/You/Names ==> Have
Example:
The Guests have arrived.
I have eaten my meal.
You have done a nice job.
Present Prefect Continuous Tense
This tense express an action that started in past and continued to present or recently stopped. It is used to state an ongoing action that has started at a point in the past. A time-reference is also used in the Sentences to show that when the action started in past or for how long the action continued.
The specific words 'since' and 'for' are used in Sentences to show the time of action. The word 'Since' is used if the exact starting time (e.g since Monday, since 3 P.M) of action is known or intended to be reflected in the Sentences. The word 'for' is used to express the amount of time (e.g for three hours, for six months) for which the action was continued.
Formula: Subject + Has been/ Have been + 1st form of Verb + ing + Object + Time-Reference
He/She/It/Name ==> Has been
I/We/You/Names ==> Have been
Example:
She has been working in an organization since 2005.
They have been waiting for me for 5 hours.
I have been watching the movie since 3 0'clock.

Past Tense

Past Simple Tense
Past Simple Tense expresses an action that occurred in past. It expresses the following type past-actions.
Formula: Subject + 2nd form of Verb + Object
He/She/It/Name/I/We/You/They/Names ==> Did
Example:
He worked in a factory
She loved her kids.
She laughed.
Past Continuous/Progressive Tense
Past Continuous Tense is used to mention an ongoing action of the past. It expresses past actions having an on-going nature. An action, that continued for some time in past, are expressed in Past Progressive Tense.
Formula : Subject + was/were + 1st form of Verb + ing + Object
I/He/She/It/Names ==> was
You/We/Names ==> were
Example:
I was drinking water
we were working
He was playing
Past Perfect Tense
Past Perfect Tense is used to express two types of actions which occurred or completed in the past. It is usually used to express an action which occurred a long time ago in past. It gives a sense of completion of an activity in past.
Formula: Subject + Had + 3rd Form of Verb + Object
He/She/It/Name/I/We/You/Names ==> Had
Example:
The Guests had arrived.
I had eaten my meal.
You had done a nice job.
Past Perfect Continuous Tense
Past Perfect Continuous Tense is used to describe an ongoing action that started in past and continued for some time in past. It expresses continued nature of an action that started and stopped at some point in the past. The Sentences include a 'time-reference' to show that when the action started in past or for how long the action was continued in past.
For time-reference of the action, two specific words 'since' and 'for' are used in the Sentences. The word 'since' is used if the exact starting time (e.g Friday, since 10 AM) of the action is known or intended to be shown in the Sentences. The word 'for' is used to expressesthe amount of time. (e.g for five hours, for nine months) for which the action was continued.
Formula: Subject + Had been + 1st form of Verb + ing + Object + Time-Reference
He/She/It/Name/I/We/You/Names ==> Had been
Example:
She had been working in an organization since 2005.
They had been waiting for me for 5 hours.
I had been watching the movie since 3 0'clock.

Future Tense

Future Simple Tense
Future Simple Tense is used to express an action that will occur or happen in the future. It is used to describe an action which will happen in near of far further after being said about it by the speaker.
Formula: Subject + will + 1st form of Verb + Object
He/She/It/Name/I/We/You/They/Names ==> will
Example:
He will work in a factory
She will love her kids.
you will work.
Future Continuous/Progressive Tense
Future Continuous Tense is used to express an on-going or continued action wich will occur at some time in the future. It expresses future actions having an on-going nature - that is expected to start in future and continue for a period of time in future.
Formula : Subject + Will be + 1st form of Verb + ing + Object
He/She/It/Names/I/You/We/Names ==> will be
Example:
I will be drinking water
we will be working
He will be playing
Future Perfect Tense
Future Perfect Tense is used to express an action which, the speaker assumes, will have completed or occurred in the future. It gives a sense of completion of a task that will happen in the future.
This tense is also sometimes used to express completion of an action in future before another activity happens.
Formula: Subject + Will have + 3rd Form of Verb + Object
He/She/It/Name/I/We/You/Names ==> Will Have
Example:
They will have brought.
I will have eaten my meal.
You will have done a nice job.
Future Perfect Continuous Tense
This tense is used to describe an ongoing action that will complete in future. It is used to express the ongoing nature of an action with regards to its continuation towards a point in future. The action is assumed to be continued for a time (specified or unspecified) in future.
A 'time-reference' is used in the Sentences to show starting time of the action or for how long the action continues.
For time-reference of the action, two specific words 'since' or 'for' are used in the Sentences. The word 'since' is used if the exact starting time (e.g. Friday, since 10 AM) of the action is known or intended to be shown in the Sentences. The word 'for' is used to express the amount of time (e.g. for five hours, for nine months) for which the action continued towards a point in future.
Formula: Subject + Will Have been + 1st form of Verb + ing + Object
He/She/It/Name/I/We/You/Names ==> Will Have been
Example:
She will have been working in an organization since 2005.
They will have been waiting for me for 5 hours.
I will have been watching the movie since 3 0'clock.
